What is Sexual Assault?

Sexual assault is when you are forced, coerced or tricked into doing non-consesual sexual things. One in every three women and one in every six men globally have been assaulted. 21% of TGQN (transgender, genderqueer, nonconforming) college students have been sexually assaulted, compared to 18% of non-TGQN females, and 4% of non-TGQN males. Some forms of sexual assault include: attempted rape, fondling or unwanted sexual touching, forcing a victim to perform sexual acts, such as oral sex or penetrating the perpetrator’s body and penetration of the victim’s body, also known as rape. If you’ve been sexually assaulted, make sure that you talk to someone who you trust and you think will understand you. It could be your friends, family or other survivors.Remember it’s not your fault and you are not alone. It’s never okay for someone to force you to do something you are uncomfortable with.
